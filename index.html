Azerbaijan

Where Europe Meets Asia

🏛️ Baku 💰 Azerbaijani Manat (AZN) 🗣️ Azerbaijani 🕐 UTC+4 ☀️ Best: Apr–Jun, Sep–Oct

⭐ Why Sell This Destination

  • Unique blend of ultramodern Baku skyline and ancient Silk Road heritage
  • Visa on Arrival for Indian passport holders — easy, fast, affordable
  • Excellent value for money — strong INR conversion
  • Perfect honeymoon & anniversary destination with luxury hotels at mid-range prices
  • Fire Temple, mud volcanoes, medieval old city — truly unique experiences
Perfect for

Couples, History Buffs, First-Time International Travellers, Luxury on a Budget

Recommended Duration

5–7 nights recommended

🌍 Destination Overview

Azerbaijan is a fascinating crossroads nation nestled between the Caucasus mountains and the Caspian Sea. Its capital Baku dazzles with futuristic flame towers rising above a UNESCO-listed medieval old city. Beyond the capital lie ancient caravanserais, mud volcanoes, fire temples, and lush green valleys. Azerbaijan offers Indian travellers an exotic yet accessible destination with easy visa access, direct flights, and outstanding value.

🗺️ Top Sights & Experiences

🏰
Old City (Icherisheher)

UNESCO-listed medieval walled city with ancient mosques and the iconic Maiden Tower.

🔥
Flame Towers

Baku's iconic futuristic skyscrapers that light up the night sky in a spectacular LED flame display.

⛩️

Ancient Zoroastrian fire temple where natural gas flames have burned for centuries.

📱Internet

💰Tipping

10% in restaurants. Round up for taxis.

Bali

Island of the Gods

🏛️ Denpasar 💰 Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) 🗣️ Bahasa Indonesia / Balinese 🕐 UTC+8 (WITA) ☀️ Best: Apr–Oct (Dry Season)

🌍 Destination Overview

Bali is Indonesia's crown jewel — a magical island where ancient Hindu temples perch on clifftops, emerald rice terraces cascade down volcanic hillsides, and the Indian Ocean delivers world-class surf. From the cultural heart of Ubud to the party beaches of Seminyak and the spiritual cliffs of Uluwatu, Bali offers something for every traveller.

Bhutan

The Last Shangri-La

🏛️ Thimphu 💰 Bhutanese Ngultrum (BTN) — INR accepted 🗣️ Dzongkha 🕐 UTC+6 ☀️ Best: Mar–May, Sep–Nov

⭐ Why Sell This Destination

  • No visa required for Indian citizens — just a permit (free!)
  • INR accepted everywhere — no currency exchange needed
  • Gross National Happiness philosophy — the world's only carbon-negative country
  • Tiger's Nest Monastery — one of the world's most iconic treks
  • Exclusive, uncrowded, pristine — the anti-mass-tourism destination
Perfect for

Spiritual Travellers, Honeymooners, Nature Lovers, Trekkers, Photography Enthusiasts

Recommended Duration

5–8 nights recommended

🌍 Destination Overview

Bhutan is the world's last great Himalayan kingdom — a land frozen in time where ancient monasteries cling to cliffsides, prayer flags flutter over mountain passes, and the Gross National Happiness index measures success. For Indian travellers, Bhutan is uniquely accessible — no visa required, INR accepted, and short flights from Kolkata, Delhi or Bengaluru.

🗺️ Top Sights & Experiences

🏔️
Tiger's Nest Monastery

Bhutan's most iconic landmark — a sacred monastery built into a 3,000ft cliff face.

🏯
Punakha Dzong

Majestic fortress-monastery at the confluence of two rivers — stunning in spring.

⛩️
Dochula Pass

High mountain pass with 108 memorial chortens and Himalayan peak views.

🛒
Thimphu Weekend Market

Vibrant farmers' market with local produce, handicrafts and authentic Bhutanese life.

☸️
Buddha Dordenma Statue

Giant 51.5m golden Buddha overlooking Thimphu valley — one of the world's largest.

🌿
Haa Valley

Remote, pristine valley — one of Bhutan's least visited gems with unspoiled nature.

🍽️ Food & Dining

Ema Datshi (chilli-cheese stew — Bhutan's national dish), Red Rice, Phaksha Paa, Momos. Vegetarian options available. Bhutanese love chilli as a vegetable, not just a spice!

ℹ️ Practical Information

💱Currency

INR is accepted everywhere. BTN is pegged 1:1 to INR. No currency hassle for Indians.

🏥Health

No mandatory vaccines. Altitude sickness possible at higher elevations.

🔌Electricity

Type D & F (230V) — Indian plugs mostly work

📱Internet

Bhutan Telecom/TashiCell SIMs available. 4G in towns, patchy in valleys.

💰Tipping

Not mandatory. BTN 200–500 for guides, drivers appreciated.

🚨Emergency

Police: 113 | Ambulance: 112 | Fire: 110

Angkor Wat

The world's largest religious monument — a breathtaking 12th-century Khmer temple complex spanning 400 acres.

Bayon Temple
